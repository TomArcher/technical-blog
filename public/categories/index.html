From Ice Shows to Algorithms: Cracking the Truck-Packing Puzzle

Aug 14, 2025 • Programming, Optimization, #Python, #Geometry, #3D Bin Packing, #Logistics, #Thought Experiment

My very first programming job was with Holiday on Ice, a touring show that hauled entire arenas’ worth of scenery, props, and gear from city to city. Every week, the crew would break everything down and load it into trucks for the next destination.

One day, the controller asked if I could write a program to figure out the most efficient way to pack those trucks. I had already built their itinerary and box-office reporting systems, so I was comfortable with logic and basic math. But this was different. This was geometry, weight distribution, and optimization rolled into one giant puzzle — and at the time, I had no idea where to start.

Should You Walk or Run in the Rain? The Puzzle That Sparked a Passion

Aug 4, 2025 • Programming, Modeling, #Python, #Modeling, #Physics, #Rain, #Weather, #Optimization, #Geometry, #Matplotlib, #Visualization, #Thought Experiment

Early in my programming career, I came across a programming problem that stuck with me for many years:

“If it’s raining, will you stay drier by walking or running through it?”

At the time, I didn’t have the skills to simulate the problem properly. It became one of the first exercises that nudged me toward a lifelong fascination with modeling the real world through code. The problem wasn’t about recursion or memory management. It was about getting wet, and how fast you move through falling rain.
